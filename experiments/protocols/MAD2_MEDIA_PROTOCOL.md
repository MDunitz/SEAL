### FeCl<sub>3</sub>·6H<sub>2</sub>O (480mM)
| **Added** | **To add (per L)** |**Substrate**| **MW** | **Long Name** | **Location** |
| :---: | :---: | :-------: |:--:| :--------------------:|:------:|
|  | 5 mL      | 1 M HCl                          | 36.46  | 1 M Hydrochloric Acid                                                | Acid Cabinet (by the gas bench) | 
|  | 6.49 g    | FeCl<sub>3</sub>·6H<sub>2</sub>O | 270.3  | Ferric chloride hexahydrate aka iron(III) chloride hexahydrate       | Chem Cabinet | 

1. Dilute 5mL of 1 M HCL in 45 mL of MQ H<sub>2</sub> (1:10 dilution) to create 50mL of 0.1 M HCL
1. Resuspend FeCl<sub>3</sub>·6H<sub>2</sub>O (ferric chloride hexahydrate aka iron(III) chloride hexahydrate) in the 0.1 M HCL
1. Vortex to mix/speed dissolution
1. Once the ferric chloride is fully dissolved filter sterilize (0.2 µm)
1. Store at 4°

#### Plates
**Prior to adding FeCl<sub>3</sub>·6H<sub>2</sub>O and Vitamin B12**
- Mix media with appropriate mass of agar (agar will not fully dissolve prior to autoclaving)
     - 1 L => 15 g 
     - 500 mL => 7.5 g
     - **400 mL => 6 g** (mix in 500 mL flask to allow for bubbling while autoclaving)
- Autoclave
- Allow liquid to cool to < 50° and add the appropriate amounts of: 
    - FeCl<sub>3</sub>·6H<sub>2</sub>O 
    - Vitamin B12
- Swirl gently to mix without creating bubbles
- Pour into plates or slats and allow to cool overnight
